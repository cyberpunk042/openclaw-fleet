## The Accountability Generator (ocf-tag)

### One-Line Definition

The Accountability Generator is an evidence-to-pressure system that transforms actions, claims, decisions, and outcomes into visible, traceable accountability structures.

### Alternate Framings

- A system that converts opaque power into trackable responsibility.
- An engine that turns facts, actors, and outcomes into accountability pressure.
- A civic-structural framework for exposing responsibility and preventing narrative escape.
- A public intelligence system for mapping who knew what, did what, and caused what.

### Core Meaning

It is not just a watchdog. It is not just reporting. It is a **pressure-and-clarity engine**.

It generates:

- Visibility
- Traceability
- Consequence mapping
- Decision exposure
- Evidence chains
- Public intelligibility
- Institutional memory
- Pressure toward correction

The word **generator** is important because the point is not merely to measure accountability, but to **produce the conditions that force it to exist**.

---

## The 5 Layers

### Layer 1: Intake

Collect:
- Claims, policies, speeches, promises
- Actions, budgets, timelines
- Incidents, outcomes
- Witnesses, evidence

### Layer 2: Structuring

Normalize into:
- Actors, institutions
- Decisions, dependencies
- Impacts, contradictions
- Timelines, confidence levels

### Layer 3: Mapping

Build:
- Responsibility chains
- Decision trees
- Knowledge graphs
- Consequence maps
- Conflict-of-interest maps
- Failure propagation maps

### Layer 4: Pressure Generation

Produce:
- Reports, public dashboards
- Contradiction flags
- Accountability scores
- Unresolved question sets
- Escalation briefs
- Timeline diffs
- Narrative-vs-reality comparisons

### Layer 5: Persistence

Ensure:
- Nothing disappears
- Edits are visible
- Claims remain linked to later outcomes
- Actors cannot reset the narrative every news cycle

This is where the word **generator** becomes very real.
